Research

I work in the Networks, Systems and Security Laboratory at UBC under the supervision of Andy Warfield and Bill Aiello.

My focus is on web scale computing, as well as advancing the HTML5 browser as a platform for rich, distributed applications. I'm also very interested in security and privacy in social networks, and on the web in general.

NSS has a proud history of building real systems and submitting work to top-tier Computer Science conferences.

Teaching

I have taught Computer Science 317 (Internet Computing) with Bill Aiello twice in past years, and a third time with Donald Acton this year (Jan - Apr, 2013.)

The course introduces the hardware and software layers of computer networks, as well as the protocols used to move data through them.

My responsibilities have included developing network programming assignments as well as teaching labs and tutorials. I recently gave a short talk on data interchange formats.

Projects

I have a number of works-in-progress, including the Yulan UI HTML5 front-end framework, as well as github.js, instagram.js and asyncimage.js. I am also currently working on a collaborative web IDE (Asper), a JavaScript x86 PC emulator (Vespen) and a small collection of natural language processing tools (NLP Tools) in Python.
