Hi, my name is Kalan. I've been coding for the web since I was a kid— it's been my favourite hobby for as long as I can remember and these days it's my vocation. I finished my BSc (Computer Science) last year and now I'm working towards the MSc. I also have the insane priviledge of working part-time at the Andreessen-Horowitz backed startup Convergent.io

Research

I work in the Networks, Systems and Security Laboratory at UBC under the supervision of Andy Warfield and Bill Aiello.

My focus is on web-scale computing, particularly around advancing the HTML5 browser as a platform for rich, distributed applications. I'm also interested in security and privacy in social networks, and on the web in general.

NSS has a proud history of building real systems and submitting work to top-tier Computer Science conferences.

Teaching

I've taught Computer Science 317 (Internet Computing) with Bill Aiello twice in past years, and a third time with Donald Acton this year (Jan - Apr, 2013.)

The course introduces the hardware and software layers of computer networks, as well as the protocols used to move data through them.

My responsibilities have included developing network programming assignments as well as teaching labs and tutorials.
