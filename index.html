Research

I work in the Networks, Systems and Security Laboratory at UBC under the supervision of Andy Warfield and Bill Aiello. My focus is on web-scale computing, particularly around advancing the HTML5 browser as a platform for rich, distributed applications. I'm also interested in security and privacy in social networks, and on the web in general. NSS has a proud history of building real systems and submitting work to top-tier Computer Science conferences.

Teaching

I've taught Computer Science 317 (Internet Computing) with Bill Aiello twice in past years, and a third time with Donald Acton this year (Jan - Apr, 2013.) The course covers the hardware and software layers of computer networks, as well as the protocols used to move data through them. I've developed network programming assignments in C and Java, as well as taught weekly labs/tutorials. Among other things, assignments have included building a web server, simulating a switch with VLAN support, and implementing IP forwarding and routing algorithms.
